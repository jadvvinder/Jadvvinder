Research & Discovery

Through extensive user interviews and workflow analysis, we identified that the fragmented tooling wasn't just an inconvenience it was actively hindering innovation. Teams were avoiding experimentation due to the perceived complexity and risk of setup errors.

üìà Lessons Learned

Internal tools deserve first-class design treatment: The success of this project demonstrated that investing in internal tool design has multiplicative effects on organizational productivity. By treating internal platforms with the same rigor as customer-facing products, we not only improved specific workflows but also established design patterns that scaled across the entire organization.

Design systems for varying expertise levels: Internal tools require special consideration for different user segments from power users needing advanced features to occasional users requiring guided simplicity. Creating adaptable components that could scale in complexity was key to adoption.

Quantitative impact drives organizational buy-in: The clear metrics around time savings and success rates were crucial for securing continued investment and expanding the design system's scope beyond the initial platform.
